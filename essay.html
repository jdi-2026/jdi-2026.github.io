Background

In the midst of global uncertainty marked by climate crisis, geopolitical tensions, technological disruption, and social-economic instability, Indonesia faces increasingly complex and multidimensional development challenges. As a country with high biological and social diversity, Indonesia confronts various structural issues ranging from climate change and deforestation, clean water crisis, health access inequality, to social and economic disparities.

These conditions demand a development approach that not only focuses on sustainability but also strengthens resilience‚Äîthe capacity of social, economic, and environmental systems to withstand, adapt, and recover from various challenges. Sustainability alone does not guarantee resilience; in an uncertain world, even sustainable systems can fail without adequate adaptive capacity and recovery mechanisms.

Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026 emerges as an intellectual space for Indonesian students in France to respond to Indonesia's development challenges through critical thinking, interdisciplinary approaches, and innovative solutions. This Call for Essay program serves as a platform for students to contribute ideas, analysis, and strategic recommendations while strengthening the role of academic diaspora in promoting Indonesia's sustainable and resilient development.

Selected essays will be compiled into a book of strategic thoughts, serving not only as academic documentation but also as a medium to deliver ideas to stakeholders, including government institutions, for reflection, discussion, and policy input.

Program Title

Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026: Problems, Insights, and Solutions from Indonesian Students in France for Indonesia

This Call for Essay program provides an intellectual platform for Indonesian students in France to contribute strategic thinking towards Indonesia's sustainable and resilient development. Selected essays will be compiled into a book and delivered to stakeholders, including government institutions, as policy input.

Purpose

The Call for Essay program "Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026: Problems, Insights, and Solutions from Indonesian Students in France for Indonesia" aims to achieve the following objectives:

  • Produce intellectual output in the form of a compiled book of essays to be delivered to stakeholders, including government institutions, as material for reflection and policy input.
  • Strengthen intellectual networks among Indonesian students in France across different educational levels and disciplines.
  • Encourage strategic thinking contributions from the academic diaspora for Indonesia's development oriented towards sustainability and resilience.

This program also serves as a platform for developing critical thinking capacity, analytical skills, and sensitivity to strategic issues in Indonesia's development, while mapping the quality of literacy and critical thinking among Indonesian students in responding to national and global challenges.

Competition Rules

Participants must adhere to the following guidelines:

  • Citizenship: Participants must be Indonesian Citizens (WNI).
  • Eligibility: Open to Indonesian undergraduate (S1), master's (S2), doctoral (S3) students, and postdoctoral researchers studying or conducting research in France.
  • Competition Categories: Essays from S1 and S2 students will be evaluated for prizes. Essays from S3 students and postdoctoral researchers can participate as thought contributions for the book compilation.
  • Word Limit: Maximum 2,000 words, excluding references.
  • Language: Essays should be written in Indonesian or English (please confirm preferred language).
  • Originality: All work must be original and demonstrate fresh perspectives, critical viewpoints, and innovative thinking.
  • Submission: Essays must be submitted through the official platform by the specified deadline.

Target Participants:

  • Indonesian Undergraduate Students (S1)
  • Indonesian Master's Students (S2)
  • Indonesian Doctoral Students (S3)
  • Indonesian Postdoctoral Researchers in France

Evaluation Criteria

Essays will be evaluated based on the following general criteria:

1. Relevance to Theme

Alignment of essay content with the Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026 theme and its connection to sustainability and resilience issues.

2. Originality of Ideas

Demonstrates fresh ideas, critical perspectives, and innovative thinking contributions.

3. Depth of Analysis

Author's ability to examine issues comprehensively, based on data, literature, and/or relevant empirical experience.

4. Structure and Logic

Clarity of essay structure, coherent flow of thought, and integration between sections of the writing.

5. Argument Clarity and Proposed Solutions

Consistency of arguments, strength of justification, and relevance and feasibility of proposed solutions or recommendations.

Key Performance Indicators

The success of the Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026 Call for Essay program will be measured through the following indicators:

  • Number of Essays Received: Target minimum of 20 essays submitted by undergraduate (S1) and master's (S2) students.
  • Cross-Level and Cross-Disciplinary Participation: Reflected through participation from S1, S2 students, as well as thought contributions from S3 students and postdoctoral researchers.
  • Number of Selected Essays for Publication: Intellectual output of the program in the form of the Indonesia Young Resilience Think Tank 2026 compilation book.
  • Dissemination of Results: Distribution of the book to relevant stakeholders, including government institutions, as material for reflection and policy input.
