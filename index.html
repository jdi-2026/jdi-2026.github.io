Background

In an increasingly uncertain world marked by climate crisis, global geopolitical tensions, technological disruption, and social-economic instability, Indonesia's development challenges have become more complex. As a nation with high biological and social diversity, Indonesia faces various structural problems ranging from climate change, deforestation, clean water crisis, health access inequality, to socio-economic disparities.

These challenges demand a development approach that focuses not only on long-term sustainability but also on strengthening national resilience‚Äîthe ability to withstand, adapt, and recover from various shocks, both from national and global dynamics. Sustainability does not always guarantee resilience; in an uncertain world, even sustainable systems can collapse without adaptive capacity and crisis recovery mechanisms.

Doctoral education in France provides Indonesian students with better access to interdisciplinary approaches, cutting-edge technology, and international collaboration that can drive sustainable solutions for Indonesia. Moreover, studying in France teaches different approaches, especially in cross-disciplinary collaboration and practical problem-solving, which are highly relevant for bringing change to Indonesia.

The Journ√©e des Doctorants Indon√©siens (JDI) 2026 brings together Indonesian doctoral students in France under the theme "From Research to Resilience: Shaping Indonesia's Future Together." This event serves as an academic discussion forum involving academics, researchers, and stakeholders from France to formulate the application of science and technology according to national needs, while encouraging research and innovation collaboration between French institutions and Indonesian doctoral students.

Purpose

JDI 2026 aims to achieve the following objectives:

  • Strengthen the Role of Research in Building Indonesia's Resilience: Provide a platform for Indonesian doctoral students to present and discuss research relevant to addressing global, social, economic, and technological challenges.
  • Articulate Strategic Problems and Solutions for Indonesia: Create intellectual space for identifying Indonesia's challenges and voicing research-based ideas, solutions, and recommendations.
  • Strengthen Multi-Sector Collaboration for Resilient Indonesia: Encourage synergy between academia, industry, government, and stakeholders in developing knowledge-based solutions.
  • Build Networks and Solidarity Among Indonesian Doctoral Students: Foster connection, expand professional networks, and strengthen camaraderie among Indonesian doctoral students in France.
  • Promote Academic and Mental Resilience: Provide space to share experiences about doctoral study dynamics, including challenges, failures, and self-doubt, to strengthen personal and collective resilience.
  • Buils Strategic Contributions from Indonesia's Academic Diaspora: Encourage active participation as academic diaspora bridging global knowledge with national needs.

Participants

The main participants are doctoral students from various disciplines in France, as well as representatives from industry and stakeholders interested in collaborating to transform research results into real innovations to support a sustainable future in Indonesia.

Format: The event will be held in hybrid format (both on-site at KBRI Paris and online via Zoom)
